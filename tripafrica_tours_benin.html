Benin

TripAfrica Tours

Benin is a French speaking West African country, a former French colony that gained it independence in 1960. Benin has four major indigenous languages; Fon, Youruba, Bariba and Dendi. Its capital is Porto-Novo, however, the seat of government is located at Cotonou, the largest city and economic capital. With a population of over 11 million people, Benin is famous for being the birthplace of the Voodoo religion, however, the Roman Catholics is the largest religious group in the country followed closely by Islam, voodoo comes next then Protestantism

Benin’s early history is barely known but it was referred to as the Slave Coast during the era of the Trans-Atlantic Slave trade stemming from the fact that a large number of slaves were shipped from this region. The historical footprints left behind by slavery has made Benin one of the most attractive places for tourists to visit to learn and experience first-hand information on Slavery. Places most visited are the city of Ouida, which is a beach that features the Door of no Return and also the Route des Escalves, the path slaves took from the Portuguese Forts to waiting ships. A memorial Arch is built on this route in memory the victims of the Slave trade.

Away from slave history, the country treats tourists to its natural attractions such as the W national park, home to amazing African animals such as cheetahs and aardvarks. Located along the Atlantic Ocean, its beautiful warm sandy beaches are perfect for relaxation and appreciation of nature’s beauty.
